Dr. Rahul Sharma is an academic leader and researcher with over 18 years of experience in higher education, research leadership, and institutional development across India, Estonia and Germany. He is recognised for strategic, outcome-driven initiatives that strengthen research capacity, academic rigor and innovation ecosystems. His work spans the development of interdisciplinary research clusters, international partnerships, digital learning platforms and institution-level transformation projects, including multi-million-euro collaborative research programmes.

Currently a Research Group Leader at the Karlsruhe Institute of Technology (KIT), Dr. Sharma previously conducted doctoral research at Tallinn University of Technology (TalTech). He has advised and collaborated with universities and institutions in Europe and India on digital education, Open & Distance Learning (ODL) programmes, and research commercialization.

### Leadership & impact
- Built and led interdisciplinary research teams in Artificial Intelligence, Cyber-Physical Systems and Intelligent Data Systems.  
- Secured and contributed to competitive international research funding and consortia.  
- Founded and scaled institutional innovation programmes (IDEA Lab), strengthening technology transfer, start-up incubation and patenting activity.  
- Advised Indian and international universities on online and blended programme design, digital pedagogy, and ODL governance aligned with NEP-2020 and UGC guidelines.

### Research interests
- Algorithmic fairness and bias in machine learning  
- Explainable and trustworthy AI  
- Role of statistical paradoxes (e.g., Simpson’s Paradox) in AI, ML and big-data applications  
- Causal inference and confounding effects in machine learning  
- Machine learning and data science methodologies  
- Generalized association rule mining and knowledge discovery
